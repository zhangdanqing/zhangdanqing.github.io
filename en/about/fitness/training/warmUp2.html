Back Training - Upper Back and Rear Deltoid (New Moves) ​

I've been struggling with a rounded shoulder and forward humerus posture issue for a long time without seeing much improvement. Recently, I saw a trainer online who mentioned that it took him five years to completely fix his forward humerus issue. Reflecting on my own training, I realized that I've only been seriously working on my upper back and shoulder girdle for about two years. It might take more intense and consistent training to overcome 20+ years of bad posture habits.

So, for my recent fitness plan focusing on back training, I need to adjust my training methods. After a thorough warm-up, I ensure my scapula is in the correct position. The main focus of the training is on the upper back, intensely working the rear deltoid, and strengthening the middle and lower trapezius/rhomboid muscles. Finally, I train the lower back.

Today, I learned a few new moves that gave me a good pump in the rear deltoid. I'll share them below.

I. High Neck Pull-Down ​

The high neck pull-down can be used as a warm-up exercise to make the shoulder girdle muscles more flexible and increase the range of motion.

  1. High Neck Pull-Down

1.1. Key Points:

  • Lean your body slightly forward, arch your back a bit, and use a medium-wide grip (since my shoulders are narrow, don't go too wide). Pull down towards the back of your neck.

1.2. Purpose:

  • If the weight is light, it's particularly suitable for warming up, making the rear deltoid and surrounding muscle groups more flexible and increasing the range of motion. If the weight is heavier, it can also work the upper back.

II. Forward High Pull-Down (Middle and Lower Trapezius) ​

Previously, I used a narrow grip for pull-downs to work the middle and lower trapezius. Now, I use a wide grip with a bar, which feels more effective. Similar to seated rows, a narrow grip works the overall muscle, while a wide grip targets the middle part more effectively. The same applies to pull-downs; a wide grip feels better for targeting the middle part.

  1. Forward High Pull-Down

1.1. Key Points:

  • Use a wider grip, lean your body back slightly, and feel the middle and lower trapezius muscles engaging.

1.2. Purpose:

  • Similar to seated rows, a narrow grip works the overall muscle, while a wide grip targets the middle part more effectively. The same applies to pull-downs; a wide grip feels better for targeting the middle part.

III. Power Rack Bent-Over Neck Pull-Down (I don't know the official name for this move) ​

Find a stable pad in the power rack to stabilize your feet and lower body. Bend over and use a medium-wide grip to pull towards the back of your neck, or pull towards the front of your neck, similar to a face pull. Pulling towards the back of the neck, I feel a particularly good pump in the rear deltoid and surrounding muscles.

  1. Power Rack Bent-Over Neck Pull-Down

1.1. Key Points:

Use this type of handle ​

Use a pad to stabilize the lower body and fix the feet position ​

Bend over in this position (not perfect yet, will adjust gradually) ​

Pull towards the back of the neck in this trajectory, or pull towards the front of the neck (similar to a face pull, don't keep your head still, tilt your head back when pulling down, and lower your head when releasing, to avoid hitting your face) ​

1.1. Purpose:

  • Pulling towards the back of the neck, I feel a particularly good pump in the rear deltoid and surrounding muscles, better than using the pec deck for reverse flyes. Pulling towards the front of the neck, I haven't quite got the feel yet; I'll try to find the feel next time.
  • The rear deltoid and surrounding muscles feel more engaged, and the upper back feels more pumped.

Today's Training Record ​

Warm-Up ​
  • Warm-up set to bring the scapula back to the normal position (reverse high pull-down + shoulder shrug 10x3, single-arm rotational press + high pull-down to send scapula 10x4)
Main Back/Rear Deltoid Workout ​
  • Close-grip seated row 10x4, wide-grip seated row 10x2
  • Close-grip high pull-down 10x4, wide-grip high pull-down 10x3
  • Power rack bent-over neck pull-down 10x3, power rack bent-over front neck pull-down 10x2
  • Pull-ups 6x2
  • Pec deck reverse flyes 10x5
  • Hyperextensions 15x4
Abdominal Serratus ​
  • Machine 15x4
Cardio ​
  • Hill climb for 30 minutes
